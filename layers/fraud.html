Layer 5: Fraud

Ingredient: Vanilla Custard with Hidden Bitter Dark Chocolate Chunks

Significance: Fraudulent sinners deceived others by misusing their gift of intelligence. Their punishments in Malebolge reflect the deceitfulness of their actions. The smooth, sweet vanilla custard appears delicious on the surface but conceals bitter, unexpected dark chocolate bits. This hidden bitterness symbolizes betrayal of trust and false appearances. When one bites into the custard expecting sweetness, they are instead met with bitterness, mirroring the false appearance of the fraudsters. The color and texture of the dark chocolate reflects the imagery of excrement and filth that the fraudsters are forced to endure. The chocolate chunks represent the deceitful actions of the fraudsters, as they are forced to eat the excrement and filth that they have caused others to endure. This ingredient highlights the deceptive nature of fraud, as it is a sin that corrupts the mind and heart of the sinner.

"The souls of the flatterers immersed in excrement that seemed to have come from human privies."
— Canto XVIII, lines 113–116
