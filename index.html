Structural Analysis

Dante arranges the circles of Hell by increasingly severe sins. The upper layers punish failures of self-control (lust, gluttony, greed). The lower layers punish more severe, deliberate sins (fraud, violence, treachery). This structure reveals the worsening nature of sins, as the upper layers lead to the lower layers. Lust causes gluttony, as one becomes so obsessed with food that they become gluttonous. Gluttony causes greed, as one hoards wealth to buy more things that they want. Greed causes violence, as people kill to get what they want. Similarly, fraud and treachery are also caused by greed, as people lie and betray to get more money, power, control, etc.

Purpose and Theme

Throughout the structure of Hell, Dante argues that sin is ultimately a willful rejection of God's love and order, which the dessert represents by increasingly unique and unexpected flavors and textures.
