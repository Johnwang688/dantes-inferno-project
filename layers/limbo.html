Layer 1: Limbo

Ingredient: Plain Whipped Cream

Significance: The virtuous pagans in Limbo committed no sin but lived without baptism or knowledge of Christ, so they suffer no physical torment. Dante depicts Limbo as a place of sighing without pain or hope of divine light. Although these pagans suffered no physical punishment, they are subject to the psycological pain of eternally longing for a place in heaven. Plain whipped cream is light and airy yet flavorless and colorless, reflecting the absence of both torment and joy. Whipped cream underscores Limbo's unique status: neither happiness nor agony, only sighing existence without hope.

"...there were no wails but just the sounds of sighs rising and trembling through the timeless air, the sounds of signs of untormented grief..."
— Canto IV, lines 25–27
